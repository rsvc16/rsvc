1. Nth Room Case

The Nth Room case was a heinous digital sex crime in South Korea that involved the blackmail and sexual exploitation of women, including minors, through encrypted chat rooms on the Telegram app. Perpetrators lured victims into performing degrading acts by threatening to expose their private information. The case shocked the nation, exposing a dark underbelly of cybercrime and leading to widespread public outrage and calls for stricter laws on digital crimes.

2. Hello Kitty Murder Case

This infamous case from Hong Kong involves the tragic murder of a nightclub hostess, Fan Man-yee, in 1999. She was abducted, tortured, and killed by a group of men over a debt dispute. Her body was dismembered, and parts of her skull were found stuffed inside a Hello Kitty doll. The gruesome details horrified the public and became a macabre symbol of unchecked violence.

3. Junko Furuta Case

One of Japan's most horrifying crimes, Junko Furuta was a high school student kidnapped by four boys in 1988. She was subjected to 44 days of unimaginable torture, including physical and sexual abuse, starvation, and humiliation, before being murdered. Her case highlighted systemic failures in Japan’s society and justice system and remains a chilling reminder of unchecked cruelty.

4. Noida Double Murder Case

In 2008, Aarushi Talwar, a 13-year-old girl, and Hemraj, the family’s domestic help, were found murdered in a wealthy Noida suburb in India. The case attracted immense media scrutiny due to its mysterious circumstances and the subsequent bungled investigation. With no clear resolution, the case spawned numerous theories ranging from honor killing to a botched robbery, leaving the truth still shrouded in mystery.

5. Twitter Killer Case

Takahiro Shiraishi, known as the "Twitter Killer," used social media to lure victims in Japan by targeting individuals with suicidal thoughts. He promised to help them die or even die alongside them. Between August and October 2017, he murdered and dismembered nine individuals. His chilling crimes revealed the dark potential of social media and prompted discussions on mental health support systems.
